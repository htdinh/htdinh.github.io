Classical applications working on image or text. * For example, very good image classification: .center[] * Solving emerging problems such as network effect (e.g. how a large crowd of people behave in specific context such as social network) --- # 2. How does it work at a high level? * **Machine Learning** * ability of a machine to learn * using large data sets **instead of hard coded rules**. * **Deep Learning**: * a subfield of machine learning * comprises of artificial neural networks. .center[] --- # 3. Why it didn't happen earlier? * Availability of huge data sets (e.g. ImageNet 14,197,122 images in 2010) * Computing power of GPU that can process large amount of data * Positive feedback loop of research that brings commercial values * Big companies pouring money into Deep Learning * Huge crowd of talents join the field that push the research boundary --- # 4. What have not been solved by DL? * "Explainable AI" - We have little explanation why the same model works for some cases and doesn't work in other cases. .center[] --- # 5. What have not been solved by DL? * Robust against adversarial attacks - For example, modification of some pixels cannot fool human eyes, but makes the algorithms fail. .center[] --- # 5. What have not been solved by DL? * Why vs what? - Success in closed question: What are there in the picture? What is the emotion (positive or negative)? - Not yet in open questions: Why people laugh? .center[] --- # 5. What have not been solved by DL? * Artificial general intelligence - Specialized efficiency at one task or similarly related tasks e.g. AlphaGo. - But not generically good as an ordinary kid who both can play decent games and can classify image. --- # Summary 1.
