__Some rules of thumb__

Of course there is no exact rule of mapping from Singular to Plural form for German nouns, but based by numbers (admittedly small data), I can _"reconfirm"_ some rules of thumb. So when facing a guess, e.g. in an exam, we can land on the more favourable side.

* `-e/-en`: "100%" substantiv, feminin ended by `-e` in Sg (43/43). If we include Maskuline and Neutrum, for every word ended by `-e` in Sg., most (92%) of them go with `-en` in Pl.. Other very rare options are `Tee, -s, Kaffee, -s, Café, -s, Knie, - , Käse, -`. (58 out of 63).

* `-/-s` (39 such words): "International" words (e.g. _Taxi, Disco_). In addition, all 7 words ended with `-o` also follow this rule `-/-s` (7 out of 7).

* Per topic, all public holidays in the list above (those are fixed in the calendar) and places for entertainment (_Kino, Theater ..._) are _Neutrum_. Of course, there are other holidays (which may be Maskuline if they ended by _-tag_) on the list, the more complete list is in Wikipedia [Gesetzliche_Feiertage_in_Deutschland](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gesetzliche_Feiertage_in_Deutschland){:target="_blank"}.
